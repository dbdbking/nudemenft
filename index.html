NudemeNFT

by Francis Lam


The NudemeNFT is a collection of 9,999 unique interactive arts living on the Ethereum blockchain. Each artwork (NFT) is made up of 1 to 2,500 programmed Nudemen, relentlessly forming a pattern, an art or a message chosen from hundreds of curated visuals.

On-chain Metadata
When you buy (mint) a NudemeNFT token, a unique set of traits (metadata) will be generated based on a block hash combined with your token ID. These traits will be stored on the Ethereum blockchain immutably. They are like the DNA of your Nudemen, which determines their behaviour, quantity, formation, how fast they move and even how hesitant they are to visualize the art that they represent.

Viewing the Artwork
After minting your token, you can find your piece on OpenSea. You can view and interact with it in any modern browser that supports Javascript (p5.js). The program to render and display the work is hosted on a decentralized file system (IPFS). The thumbnail on OpenSea will be updated before the collection is sold out.

How to Buy/Mint
Currently, the NudemeNFT can only be minted directly from our smart contract on Etherscan with a discounted price of 0.05 ETH (0.09 ETH for public sale). Up to 5 tokens can be minted for each transaction. Minting from a contract requires technical understanding of how smart contracts and Ethereum blockchain work. Please only proceed if you know what you’re doing.

Withheld Tokens
50 tokens (#1 - #50) were minted at the contract deployment and are being withheld for the artist, team and promotional purposes. These tokens were generated randomly on the blockchain based on the same algorithm as the others.

About The Nudemen
The Nudemen Series is an ongoing art project that aims to raise sociological questions in the post-Internet era. The project was originally inspired and created to counterbalance the overwhelming female nudity over the Internet (Nudemessenger, 2003). The series has taken an utopian approach to depict technology, culture and reality in different forms and media.

The work has been awarded and exhibited in multiple venues such as Ars Electronica, Japan Media Arts Festival, New Vision Arts Festival, Apple App Store Featured and CreativeApplications.
